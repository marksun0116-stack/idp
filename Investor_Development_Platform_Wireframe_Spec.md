# Investor Development Platform UI Wireframe Specification

## Overview

This wireframe represents the primary dashboard experience for self-directed investors. The design philosophy combines elements of Strava, GitHub, and modern fintech applications. The focus is investor improvement rather than stock monitoring.

---

# 1. Global Navigation (Left Sidebar)

Purpose:
Provide consistent navigation throughout the platform.

Sections:

## Dashboard
Investor home page.

## Research
Watchlists, stock research, signal validation, and AI research tools.

## Decisions
Investment decision journal and decision history.

## Portfolios
Strategy portfolios and portfolio analytics.

## Reviews
Scheduled decision reviews and lessons learned.

## Analytics
Behavioral analytics and investor improvement metrics.

## Community
Investor communities, discussions, and shared strategies.

## Profile
Public investor profile and reputation metrics.

## Settings
Account and application settings.

---

# 2. Top Header

Purpose:
Provide global search and user controls.

Components:

## Welcome Message
Personalized greeting.

Example:
"Welcome back, Mark"

## Search Bar
Search stocks, portfolios, strategies, investors, and decisions.

## Notifications
Upcoming reviews, alerts, and community activity.

## User Avatar
Account access and profile shortcuts.

---

# 3. Investor Scorecards

Purpose:
Provide an immediate snapshot of investor progress.

## Decision Quality Score (DQS)

Primary platform metric.

Measures:

- Research quality
- Decision discipline
- Risk management
- Strategy consistency
- Outcome quality

Displayed:

- Current score
- Trend indicator
- Change since previous period

---

## Research Discipline

Measures:

- Research completeness
- Evidence collection
- Thesis documentation

---

## Risk Management

Measures:

- Position sizing
- Diversification
- Portfolio concentration
- Constraint adherence

---

## Behavioral Score

Measures:

- Emotional discipline
- FOMO tendencies
- Loss aversion
- Consistency

---

# 4. Recent Activity Feed

Purpose:
Show investor actions and platform activity.

Examples:

- Reviewed NVDA thesis
- Added AMD to watchlist
- Completed portfolio review
- Created new investment decision
- Updated strategy portfolio

Design Goal:
Create momentum and engagement.

---

# 5. Performance Overview

Purpose:
Track performance over time.

Features:

- Strategy performance
- Benchmark comparison
- Historical trends

Benchmarks:

- S&P 500
- NASDAQ 100
- User strategies

Time Filters:

- 1 Month
- 3 Months
- YTD
- 1 Year
- All Time

---

# 6. Upcoming Reviews

Purpose:
Maintain investor learning loops.

Displays:

- 30-day reviews
- 90-day reviews
- 180-day reviews
- Annual reviews

Each item contains:

- Decision title
- Review type
- Due date
- Review status

---

# 7. Active Strategies

Purpose:
Display currently managed strategy portfolios.

Example Strategies:

- AI Infrastructure
- Dividend Growth
- Cloud Software
- Healthcare Innovation

Displayed Metrics:

- Current value
- Total return
- Trend chart
- DQS contribution

---

# 8. Behavioral Insights

Purpose:
Deliver actionable investor coaching.

Examples:

Positive:

- Research quality improving
- Diversification improving
- Better position sizing

Warnings:

- Holding losers too long
- Excess concentration
- Chasing momentum

This section is one of the most important differentiators in the platform.

---

# 9. Decision Funnel

Purpose:
Visualize investor process maturity.

Stages:

## Ideas Captured
Potential opportunities identified.

## Researched
Evidence collected.

## Decisions Made
Formal decision records created.

## Positions Opened
Investment executed.

## Reviewed
Outcome reviewed and lessons captured.

Key Metric:

Decision-to-review completion rate.

---

# 10. Market Snapshot

Purpose:
Provide lightweight market context.

Displayed:

- S&P 500
- NASDAQ 100
- Dow Jones
- VIX
- Treasury Yield

Design Goal:

Inform investors without overwhelming them.

This is intentionally smaller than traditional finance applications.

---

# 11. Recent Decisions Table

Purpose:
Provide visibility into active decision records.

Columns:

- Ticker
- Decision Type
- Confidence
- Status
- Return
- Creation Date

Example:

NVDA
Buy
Confidence 8/10
Active
+12.5%

---

# 12. Community Decisions

Purpose:
Expose users to high-quality investor thinking.

Shows:

- Popular decisions
- High-quality theses
- Top-performing strategies

Ranking Factors:

- Decision Quality Score
- Research quality
- Community engagement
- Long-term outcomes

Not ranked solely by returns.

---

# Design Philosophy

The dashboard is centered around:

Investor
→ Strategy
→ Decision
→ Evidence
→ Outcome
→ Learning

This differs from traditional finance applications that focus primarily on:

Stock
→ Price
→ Chart

The primary goal is to help investors improve decision quality and build long-term investing credibility.
